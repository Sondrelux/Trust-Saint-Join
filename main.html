Success transition

Kari has been employed in Equinor for 30 years, working offshore as a process engineer. She excelled in optimizing refining processes and ensuring operational efficiency while minimizing environmental impact.

Driven by her passion for sustainability, Emily made a deliberate career shift towards renewable energy. Joining a leading renewable energy company, she became a valuable asset to the team focusing on wind turbine design and manufacturing.

In her new role, Emily utilized her process engineering skills to enhance manufacturing processes for wind turbines. Collaborating with a diverse team, she streamlined assembly lines, improved quality control measures, and championed sustainable practices from material sourcing to waste reduction.

Emily's contributions extended beyond manufacturing. She actively participated in research and development, exploring innovative approaches to enhance turbine performance through advanced materials and cutting-edge techniques.

Through her dedication and expertise, Emily played a pivotal role in advancing renewable energy technology, contributing to a greener and more sustainable future.

In a professional manner, Emily's career transition from the oil industry to renewable energy showcased her commitment to sustainability and her ability to leverage her process engineering skills for the benefit of the environment.
